# About me

When I was born in 1994, I had no idea I would one day become an engineer. Growing up in a modest family in India — my father a school teacher, my mother a homemaker — I was a sickly child who spent most of his time at home. But being homebound turned into an unexpected gift: I became a self-learner, always finishing in the top 1 or 2 of my class.

I learned what understanding truly means when my science teacher told me during an exam: *"Just write what you understand in your own words."* I scored 95%. That day I decided to never memorize blindly — instead, understand, learn, and push forward. The results will follow.

I learned what self-reliance means when my bicycle was stolen just five days after I finally got one. That day I decided: what you earn stays with you — what comes free, doesn't.

My father wanted me to become a teacher, but I always wanted to be an engineer. Against all odds — financial hardships, entrance exams, and my mother pledging her only gold to fund my PG Diploma at CDAC Kolkata — I made it. I completed my Electronics and Communication Engineering degree and earned a Post Graduate Diploma in Embedded Systems Design with Grade A.

My first job at Continental Automotive in the Vehicle Dynamics business unit started a career that would take me from Graduate Engineer Trainee to Technical Architect in six years. I automated resource analysis processes, built worldwide databases for control function resources, integrated GLIWA T1 timing analysis, and received 15 spot awards for exceptional work.

In November 2022, I moved to Germany and joined Marquardt GmbH as an Advanced Software Development Engineer. Here I design, develop, and maintain core tools for the SPLE platform, conduct training sessions, and help colleagues through our support channel.

I am interested in software product line engineering, build systems, automation, and continuous learning through coding dojos and katas.
