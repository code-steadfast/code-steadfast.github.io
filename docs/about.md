# About me

**A self-taught engineer who went from borrowing bicycles to building software platforms used worldwide.**

---

I was born in 1994 into a modest family in India. My father, a private school teacher, never earned much — but he earned something far more valuable: the respect of everyone around him. My mother held our family together with quiet strength. Growing up alongside my elder brother and younger sister, I learned early what resilience looks like.

I was a sickly child who spent most of his time at home. But being homebound became an unexpected gift — I became a self-learner, always finishing in the top 1 or 2 of my class.

## The lesson that shaped my life

During a science exam, I was too sick to recall anything I had studied. I sat there crying, convinced I would fail. Then my teacher walked over and said:

> *"You are in my class. You remember what I taught you. Just write what you understand in your own words. I don't care about grammatical mistakes — if I can understand what you wrote, I will give you marks."*

I scored 95%. That day I learned: **never memorize blindly — understand, learn, and push forward. The results will follow.**

This has been my life motto ever since.

## What a stolen bicycle taught me

Growing up, money was always tight. I always wanted a bicycle but could never afford one. My father promised me one if I scored above 500 in my 10th exams. I scored 499.

A year later, he finally managed to get me one. But I fell sick for over a month. When I finally rode it — just five days later, it was stolen.

That day I decided: **I will earn everything myself. What you earn stays with you — what comes free, doesn't.**

## Engineering against all odds

My father wanted me to become a teacher, but I always wanted to be an engineer. I told him: *either I do engineering, or I stay home and you feed me.* A family friend stepped in with money for the entrance exam, and I cleared it.

The government introduced fee reimbursement that year. Friends of my father helped with books and fees. From the 3rd year, I worked part-time evenings and weekends to fund myself. I also started teaching my classmates — Analog Communications, Control Systems, Embedded Systems, Signal Processing, Microprocessors — with the support of my professors. I graduated in Electronics and Communication Engineering.

## The scariest bet of my life

After graduation, I found my dream course: a PG Diploma in Embedded Systems at CDAC Kolkata. But it cost ₹80,000. A family friend helped with ₹25,000. For the rest, my mother gave the only gold she had to secure a bank loan.

If I had failed, that gold — our family's only emergency reserve — would have been gone forever.

I moved to Kolkata, stayed with relatives, and completed the program with **Grade A**. The bet paid off.

## Continental Automotive (2016–2022)

I landed my first job through a grueling 12-hour interview at Continental Automotive — one written test, two technical rounds, and HR — competing against 40 candidates. I joined the Vehicle Dynamics business unit as a Graduate Engineer Trainee.

Over six and a half years, I grew from trainee to Technical Architect:

*Graduate Engineer Trainee → Associate Engineer → Executive Engineer → Senior Engineer → Technical Architect*

My work centered on **software resource management and timing analysis** for automotive braking systems. I automated the entire milestone analysis process using Perl, built a worldwide database of control function resources by testing on IPG and dSPACE HILs, and took global responsibility for resource estimation across India, China, Japan, Korea, Germany, and the USA — helping determine which microcontrollers to use for projects like Ford and BMW.

I integrated GLIWA T1 timing analysis into the RTM component for Vector OS, spending 3 months in Frankfurt to support vision control software timing. Later, I built Python applications to extract and analyze timing data — my first step into the language that would define my next chapter.

Along the way, I saved a BMW project through compiler and linker optimization, led a Git migration for a 40-member team, mentored a plant manager in Delhi, and trained multiple colleagues as my deputies. I received **15 spot awards** and consistently exceeded performance expectations.

## Marquardt GmbH, Germany (2022–present)

In November 2022, I moved to Germany and joined **Marquardt GmbH** as an **Advanced Software Development Engineer** in the Rhine-Main Team.

Here, I found my stride in software platform engineering. I design, develop, and maintain core tools for the SPLE (Software Product Line Engineering) platform — including spl-core, pypeline, hammocking, and bootstrap.

Some highlights: I introduced automated RAM/ROM reporting into the build process — something that didn't exist before — giving project teams visibility into resource consumption for the first time. With a colleague, I built a framework that automated post-processing artifact generation, replacing numerous manual scripts and saving roughly a day of work per project. I also developed custom Python tools that completely replaced expensive Vector license dependencies for ODX/PDX/OUC and vFlashpack generation, directly cutting costs for the company.

I've co-conducted **10+ SPLE platform training sessions**, and I actively monitor our support channel to help colleagues quickly — something the team and management have recognized me for. Most recently, I co-introduced AI-assisted development workflows that teams have already adopted, achieving up to 85% code coverage on legacy components in a single day.

## Beyond work

When I'm not coding, you'll find me on the move — running, swimming, inline skating, ice skating, hiking, or bouldering. I recently discovered skiing and loved it. I'm also an anime enthusiast, and when the creative mood strikes, I draw and paint. Cooking is another passion.

I stay sharp through coding dojos and katas, and collaborate with colleagues on side projects to keep learning.

**My goal?** To one day become a CTO. It's a long road — but I believe in the same motto that got me here: *keep learning, push forward, and the results will come.*
